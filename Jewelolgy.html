Gold Facts

Gold is a precious and Noble metal. It has an excellent chemical stability and high resistance to oxidation and corrosion. The purity of gold is measured in carats where pure gold is 24 carats; henceforth

  • 24 carat has 99.5% of gold content
  • 23 carat has 95.83% of gold content
  • 22 carat has 91.6% of gold content
  • 18 carat has 75% of gold content

Until recently only impure gold jewellery used to be sold in India wherein the joint s and hooks in jewellery were made with lower karatage of gold known as tanka.with every single unit of underkaratage, the customer looses bulk of money. But lately ,after the introduction of KDM most retailers have begun to sell pure karatage gold which is certyified or hallmarked by a BIS laboratory.


Hallmarked Jewellery

Hallmarked Jewellery may seem expensive due to high making charges but the customer eventually saves money because he does not buy impure gold at the price of gold. Therefore, always check for hallmarking stamp before buying gold jewellery.


Kundan Facts

Kundan jewellery is one of the oldest forms of jewellery made and worn in India. Kundan received great patronage during the Mughal era and many stunning pieces were created in those times. Kundan work is method of gem setting, consisting of inserting gold foil between the stones and its mount. Kundan jewellery is a product of well orchestrated team work and involves a variety of specialist skills; the designer (naqash,chitera), the goldsmith (sonar), the engraver (thegharaiiwala), the enameller (minakar), the gem setter (kundansaz) and stringer (patua).
